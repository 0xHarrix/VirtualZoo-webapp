THE LION

Lions, known as the "king of the jungle," are majestic creatures belonging to the cat family, Felidae. They are apex predators in their habitat, primarily inhabiting grasslands, savannas, and open woodlands in Africa. Lions live in social groups called prides, consisting of related females, their cubs, and a few adult males. With impressive manes and powerful physiques, lions primarily rely on their keen eyesight and stealth to ambush prey, which includes large mammals such as buffalo, zebra, and wildebeest. Despite being apex predators, lions face threats such as habitat loss, human-wildlife conflict, and poaching. Conservation efforts are crucial to protect lions and ensure their survival for future generations.
